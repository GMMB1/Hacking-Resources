Here are some valuable websites for reconnaissance and bug hunting:

---

**Subdomain & DNS Enumeration**
- https://dnsdumpster.com/ — DNS recon and subdomain finder
- https://crt.sh/ — Certificate transparency logs, great for finding subdomains
- https://securitytrails.com/ — DNS history and subdomain enumeration
- https://virustotal.com/ — Scan URLs/IPs and find related subdomains

**OSINT & Information Gathering**
- https://shodan.io/ — Search engine for internet-connected devices
- https://censys.io/ — Similar to Shodan, finds exposed services
- https://whois.domaintools.com/ — WHOIS lookup
- https://www.exploit-db.com/google-hacking-database — Google dorks database

**Email & People**
- https://hunter.io/ — (you already have this)
- https://haveibeenpwned.com/ — Check if emails were in data breaches
- https://www.social-searcher.com/ — Social media OSINT

**Vulnerability & Exploit Research**
- https://www.exploit-db.com/ — Exploit database
- https://nvd.nist.gov/ — National vulnerability database (CVEs)
- https://cve.mitre.org/ — CVE lookup

**Web Application Testing**
- https://webhook.site/ — Capture HTTP requests (great for XSS/SSRF testing)
- https://requestbin.com/ — Similar to webhook.site
- https://portswigger.net/web-security — Free Web Security Academy labs

**Hash & Encoding**
- https://crackstation.net/ — (you already have this)
- https://hashes.com/ — Hash cracking
- https://cyberchef.org/ — All-in-one encoding/decoding/analysis tool (very powerful!)

**Wordlists & Payloads**
- https://github.com/danielmiessler/SecLists — The best collection of wordlists for fuzzing and brute forcing

---

**CyberChef** and **SecLists** are especially must-haves for anyone doing penetration testing and bug hunting. 🔥
